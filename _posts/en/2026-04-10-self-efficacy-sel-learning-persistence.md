## Where Does Learning Motivation Decline Begin?

Many students experience a decline in learning motivation during their academic journey. However, this is not simply a matter of willpower. Recent research shows that **Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)** enhances self-efficacy and fundamentally transforms student motivation and learning persistence.

![Student studying in a classroom](/assets/images/self-efficacy-sel-learning-persistence/01.jpg)

## From Emotional Awareness to Self-Efficacy

According to CASEL research, developing social-emotional competencies goes beyond simply understanding emotions. This process unfolds in the following stages:

**Stage 1: Emotional Awareness**
The first step is accurately identifying your own emotional state. It begins with the question: "What am I feeling right now?"

**Stage 2: Self-Regulation**
As Cipriano and colleagues (2023) demonstrate in their research, the ability to appropriately manage emotions after recognizing them is crucial. This enables students to manage frustration when facing learning challenges and maintain focus.

**Stage 3: Rising Self-Efficacy**
The experience of being able to control one's emotions develops into the belief that "I can do this." This is self-efficacy.

## Learning Persistence Begins With Emotional Competence

The evidence that SEL improves student motivation, attitudes, and learning persistence is clear. The key emphasis in research is this:

> "SEL improves self-efficacy, motivation, and perseverance."

When students better understand and manage their emotions:
- Learning motivation naturally increases
- Attitudes toward challenging tasks become more positive
- Resilience to try again after failure develops
